Overview: What is Networking and Why should I do it?

Networking is the process of building and maintaining professional relationships that can support your career growth. It involves connecting with individuals in your field, learning from their experiences, and sharing your own journey. Effective networking can lead to job opportunities, mentorship, collaborations, and a deeper understanding of your industry.
By building these relationships, you can gain access to exclusive opportunities, receive guidance from experienced professionals, and stay informed about industry trends.

Here's are few places to reach out to:

  • Attend Events
    Participate in UMSI career fairs, workshops, and industry meetups to connect with professionals and peers.

  • Leverage Alumni Networks
    Reach out to UMSI alumni for advice, mentorship, and potential job leads.

  • Utilize Social Media
    Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with industry professionals, join relevant groups, and share your work.

  • Informational Interviews
    Request informational interviews to learn more about specific roles or companies and expand your network.

Step 1: Be Prepared

Before you start networking, it's important to be prepared. This includes having a clear understanding of your goals, preparing an elevator pitch, and gathering relevant information about the professionals you want to connect with. Being prepared will help you make a strong impression and have meaningful conversations.

Here are some Must-haves and Good-to-haves:

Must-haves:

  • An Up-to-date Resume

  • A Well-Prepared Self-Introduction

  • Questions You're Curious About

  • Be Prepared to Share Your Work - Portfolio or Worksamples

Good-to-haves:

  • Portfolio or work samples

  • Notes on industry trends or news

  • List of questions for informational interviews

Step 2: Reach Out

Once you're prepared, it's time to reach out to professionals in your field. This can be done through various channels such as LinkedIn, email, or attending events. The key is to be genuine and respectful of their time.

Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Be clear and concise in your outreach messages.

  • Ask to chat, not a job.

  • Personalize your messages to show genuine interest.

  • Follow up appropriately without being pushy.

Step 3: Follow Up

After a successful conversation, it's important to follow up. This shows your interest and professionalism. A brief follow-up message can go a long way in building a relationship.

Here are a few ways to do it:

  • Send a thank-you email expressing your appreciation for their time, also recapping key points from your conversation.

  • Mention any next steps you discussed, such as sending additional information or scheduling another meeting.

  • Connect on LinkedIn if you haven't, and send the follow-up message through that.

Step 4: Maintain Your Network

Networking is not a one-time activity; it's an ongoing process. Regularly engage with your contacts, share updates, and offer support when possible. Maintaining your network will ensure that you have a strong support system throughout your career.

Here are why it matters:

  • It helps you stay connected with professionals in your field.

  • It allows you to learn about new opportunities and trends.

  • It builds trust and relationships that can last throughout your career.

Step 5: A Digital Presence

In this day and time, having a strong professional online presence is crucial as a new grad. This includes maintaining an updated LinkedIn profile, participating in relevant online communities & discussions, and showcasing your work through a personal website or portfolio.

The Benefits of a strong digital presence:

  • It gives you exposure to potential employers and recruiters.

  • It allows you to showcase your skills and accomplishments.

  • It helps you connect with professionals globally.
